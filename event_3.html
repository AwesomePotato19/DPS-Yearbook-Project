Online Learning VS Onsite Learning

Blog by Arnav Anup

In 2020, The start of a global pandemic called as COVID-19 forced us to accept a new “online” world as new normal. We had to find alternative ways to live our daily lives but with the new challenge of COVID. Schools and offices now had to operate using zoom, teams or any other video conferencing app.

After more than a year of this method of studying and working, educators and students have become more thorough with the usage of technology and have become more capable of using technology for daily life. However, there is a lot that comes when we have to choose which method is better. Let us find out.

Onsite Learning

Pros

Establishes a relationship between the teacher and the student; In a classroom, learners and teachers can spend more in-person time together.

You can avail different types of learning methods, including auditory, visual, tactical, etc.

Promotes networking among the learners; A classroom is a place where peers can meet different people, make new friends and cultivate social skills.

Direct and real-time interaction between learners and teachers; More importantly, teachers can instantly answer your questions.

Cons

A classroom requires social contact. Given the ongoing pandemic, this puts each student's safety at risk.

Classroom learning is not flexible. Moreover, the teacher as well as the progress of other students can affect the pace of your learning.

There may be multiple interruptions inside a classroom. Additionally, pointless conversations or debates can take place which would potentially disturb the learning process.

Online learning is a type of learning where learning is transferred through online means. This style of learning has grown to become extremely popular in recent times despite its prevalence prior to the pandemic. Certificate courses are also available online where there is no need to enter a classroom to get certified.

Online Learning

Pros

In the midst of the ongoing pandemic, online learning enables learners to socially distance and stay safe.

You can learn at your own pace. In other words, your learning pace is not affected by the teacher or the progress of other students.

You have the freedom to learn from a location that suits you as long as you have access to WiFi. Moreover, you can even attend a class while you're on vacation in a foreign country with a different time zone.

Similar to online shopping, online learning enables you to select what you want to learn from a wide range of options. For instance, you can find a graphic design course at multiple sources. In addition, your choice can be based on your needs.

Cons

Online learning limits social networking among the learners due to lack of human interaction.

You can take part in online learning only with access to strong WiFi. However, without access to the Internet, there isn't much you can do.

Courses that require a physical setup such as driving, swimming and public speaking can never take place online.

Online learning requires extra determination and self-motivation to keep up with classes and stay on track. Negative habits or low motivation can cause some learners to fall behind.

Conclusion:

Finally, it is important to remember that the transition from onsite learning to online learning is not easy. It is a different experience for each individual. Classroom Learning involves physical interactions with a teacher and peers, while online learning transfers this component to a virtual environment. So, while there is human interaction, it happens online via virtual lectures, virtual discussions, face-to-face video workshops etc.
